About SASC

SASC — Symbolic Comparative Analysis System — is a methodology created to reconstruct, compare, and interpret historical, symbolic, and scientific patterns present in human traditions.

By cross-referencing multiple sources — archaeological, historical, folkloric, cultural, and symbolic or unconventional records — SASC identifies convergences, traces, and recurring patterns, reconstructing pieces of a history forgotten or distorted by the official narrative.

Why it was created

We live in an era where science often walks isolated from other forms of knowledge. Where official history ignores the symbolic, and where many ancestral, cultural, and non-conventional forms of knowledge are discarded simply because they do not fit into traditional academic validation models.

SASC emerges as a bridge. A methodology that respects both objective data and the symbolic and cultural traces left over millennia. A map for those who seek to understand the invisible, decipher patterns, reconstruct narratives, and realize that the truth is often hidden between the lines.

How it works

SASC applies a method based on:

  • Assigning weights to sources.
  • Analyzing recurrences and symbolic convergences.
  • Checking the geographical spread of symbols and patterns.
  • Mandatory weight alternation to eliminate bias and maximize interpretive neutrality.
  • Generating symbolic maps, hypotheses, and structured interpretations.

Who it is for

  • Independent researchers.
  • Alternative historians.
  • Scientists open to integrating non-conventional data.
  • Scholars of the symbolic, metaphysical, and occult.
  • Anyone interested in understanding the truth beyond the official narrative.
  • Spiritual seekers.
