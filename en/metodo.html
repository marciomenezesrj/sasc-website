SASC Method — Symbolic Comparative Analysis System

The 6 Sources of SASC

  • 🏺 Archaeology and material evidence.
  • 📖 Official history and academic records.
  • 🌍 Folklore, myths, and oral traditions.
  • 🔮 Channelings, mediumship, and non-conventional records.
  • 🧘 Spiritual, esoteric, and universal symbolic knowledge.
  • 🗺️ Cultural and linguistic trace.

Operational Pillars

  • ✔️ Assignment of Weights.
  • ✔️ Alternation of Weights.
  • ✔️ Analysis of Recurrences.
  • ✔️ Multiplication of Geographic Recurrence.
  • ✔️ Mapping of Convergences.
  • ✔️ Recording of Divergences.

The pillar of Multiplication of Geographic Recurrence allows symbolic patterns present in different regions and cultures to have their weight amplified, reflecting their robustness as universal symbolic evidence.

“SASC does not offer ready-made answers. It offers a map. Because truth is not a fixed point. It is a pattern that repeats over time, in culture, in history, and on the very planet.”
